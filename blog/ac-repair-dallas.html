Frequently Asked Questions — AC Repair Dallas

Why does my AC blow cold on the highway but warm in Dallas city traffic?

This is one of the most common AC complaints we hear in Dallas. The most likely cause is a low refrigerant charge — at highway speeds the system maintains enough pressure to cool, but at idle it can't keep up with the heat load. A condenser fan that isn't running is another culprit. Either way, we need to diagnose why cooling drops at idle before just adding refrigerant.

How much does an AC recharge cost at CarNation Elite?

The recharge itself is relatively inexpensive, but the total cost depends on what's causing the low refrigerant. If there's no leak and the system is simply low due to normal permeation over time, a recharge is straightforward. If there's a leak — which is far more common — we need to locate and repair it first, or the refrigerant will escape again within weeks or months. We'll tell you upfront what we find and what it will cost to fix it correctly.

How often should I have my car's AC serviced in Dallas?

Most manufacturers suggest checking AC performance every 2 years. In North Texas, where AC runs hard for 5–6 months a year, we see systems degrade faster than in cooler climates. If your AC feels weaker than last summer, have it checked in April or May before the heat peaks — catching a small refrigerant loss early is far cheaper than a seized compressor in July.

My AC has a musty smell when I turn it on. Is that dangerous?

The smell itself is typically not harmful, but evaporator mold does indicate the system needs attention. It develops when moisture doesn't drain properly or the AC isn't run long enough to dry out after use. We clean the evaporator, treat it to inhibit regrowth, and replace the cabin air filter — that combination usually eliminates the odor completely.

What causes AC compressors to fail in Dallas heat?

The most common immediate cause is low refrigerant — refrigerant also carries the compressor lubricant, so a compressor running low is running without adequate lubrication and will seize or burn out. Electrical failures and worn clutch assemblies are also common after years of Texas-season use. If your AC stops cooling suddenly rather than gradually, don't keep cycling it on and off — bring it in.
