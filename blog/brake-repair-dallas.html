Signs You Need Brake Repair

  • High-pitched squealing or squeaking when you first apply the brakes — especially on cold mornings before the rotors warm up
  • Grinding or metal-on-metal sound during braking — your pads are worn through the friction material to the metal backing plate
  • Vibration or pulsing through the brake pedal when slowing down, especially noticeable at highway speeds on 635 or I-30
  • Vehicle pulling left or right when you brake — can mean a stuck caliper, uneven pad wear, or a collapsed brake hose
  • Soft or spongy pedal that travels further toward the floor than it used to before you feel braking pressure

Frequently Asked Questions — Brake Repair Dallas

How often should Dallas drivers get their brakes inspected?

We recommend a brake inspection at every oil change — roughly every 5,000–7,500 miles. Dallas stop-and-go traffic and summer heat accelerate brake pad wear compared to cooler climates. Catching low pads before they reach metal-to-metal is far cheaper than replacing scored rotors. We measure pad thickness at every inspection and tell you exactly how much life you have remaining so you can plan ahead.

Do I need new rotors when I replace brake pads?

Not always. If rotors are still within manufacturer minimum thickness specifications and show no significant scoring or heat warping, they can be resurfaced. If they're already at or near minimum thickness, replacement is the smarter investment — thin rotors are more prone to warping from heat, won't survive another resurface, and won't give you the stopping performance the new pads are capable of. We measure and show you the numbers before recommending anything.

Is it safe to drive to your shop with grinding brakes?

Grinding typically means your pads are worn through and metal is contacting the rotor face. If the vehicle is still stopping and the pedal feels firm and consistent, you can generally drive it carefully to our shop. If the pedal is soft, pulls hard to one side, or the grinding is severe and sudden, call us first — a tow may be safer than risking a braking failure in Dallas traffic.

How long does a brake job take at CarNation Elite?

A front or rear brake pad and rotor replacement on a typical passenger vehicle takes 1–2 hours. All four corners with caliper service and a brake fluid flush runs 2.5–3.5 hours. We'll give you an accurate time estimate when you drop off, and we'll call immediately if anything changes once we're into the job.

Why is my brake pedal soft or spongy?

A soft or spongy pedal almost always points to air in the brake lines, contaminated brake fluid that has absorbed moisture and is compressible, or a fluid leak somewhere in the system. In severe cases it can indicate a failing master cylinder. This is a safety condition — a pedal that doesn't give you solid, consistent braking resistance should be diagnosed and repaired before your next drive.
