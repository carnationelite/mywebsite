What We Fix

Common Warning Signs of Car Electrical Failure

Electrical problems can range from a simple blown fuse to a complex ECU fault. Our ASE-certified technicians diagnose and repair them all.

Battery & Charging Problems

Frequent dead batteries, alternator failures, or a dashboard battery warning light that won't go off.

Starting Issues

Clicking sounds when turning the key, slow cranking, or an engine that refuses to start at all.

Lighting & Accessory Malfunctions

Dim or flickering headlights, stuck power windows, non-responsive locks, or repeatedly blown fuses.

Complex Wiring & Sensor Issues

Modern vehicles rely on dozens of sensors. We diagnose short circuits, faulty ECUs, and failed control modules.

Dashboard Warning Lights

Check engine, ABS, or airbag lights triggered by electrical faults — we read codes and fix the root cause, not just clear them.

AC & Electrical Accessories

Blower motor failures, rear defroster issues, or a malfunctioning infotainment system traced to wiring faults.

Auto Electrical Repair FAQs

Common questions Dallas drivers ask before bringing their vehicle in.

How much does it cost to check a car's electrical system?
A standard electrical diagnostic fee varies depending on the complexity of the issue, but it ensures we target the exact problem without wasting money on unnecessary parts. We're typically 20–40% below dealer pricing and will give you a clear, itemized estimate before any work begins. Call (214) 597-4922 for a quick quote specific to your vehicle.
Can a bad alternator destroy a brand-new car battery?
Yes — absolutely. If your alternator is overcharging or undercharging, it can quickly drain or permanently damage a healthy battery. This is why we always test both the alternator and the battery together during every electrical diagnosis, ensuring you don't pay to replace one only to have the other destroy it.
How long does an electrical diagnostic take?
Simple battery or starter tests take as little as 15–30 minutes. Tracing a complex hidden wiring short or an intermittent ECU fault can take 1 to 2 hours. We keep you updated every step of the way and won't start repair work without your approval.
Do you work on all vehicle makes and models?
Yes. Our technicians are trained and equipped to diagnose and repair electrical issues on domestic and import vehicles — cars, trucks, and SUVs. Whether you drive a Ford, Toyota, BMW, Chevy, or anything in between, we have the tools and expertise to help.
What warranty do you offer on electrical repairs?
Every repair at CarNation Elite is backed by a 12-month / 12,000-mile warranty on parts and labor. If anything fails within that window, we fix it at no charge. We also provide a written estimate before any work begins — you're in full control of every decision.
